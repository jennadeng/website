History of Burma

The Republic of the Union of Myanmar, formerly known as Burma, is a sovereign state in Southeast Asia. With current population over 50 million and neighboring countries such as India, China, Thailand, Laos, and Bangladesh, Myanmar stands as one of the oldest and ancient countries.

On Burmese Food

Burmese cuisine is a fusion between Indian and Thai food flavors. However one of the most signature dish is Tea Leave Salad. Tea Leave is called “laphat” which plays a significant role in Myanmar society. Fermented tea leave is made best in the cold Northern mountain of Shan state region in Myanmar. Tea Leave salad is eaten best when paired with fried assorted beans and dried shrimp. It is also known to be an ancient symbolic peace offering between warring kingdoms. It is also traditionally provided at every house when visitors come. Now it can be found at any authentic Burmese restaurants.

About BeyondBurma

Beyond Burma was created with passion for food and inspiration to share Burmese culture in the most diverse city of San Francisco. When the founder came to US 18 years ago, she had only one thing in mind. It was to live American dream by running her own business and introducing delicious Burmese cuisine. Little did she knows, Burmese food was already popular and well liked by everyone who had tried in this city. However, she found that there was more demand than the supply and most restaurants and vendors keep it very traditional. So she and a business decided to create something more authentic but creative and add a twist to their fun menu. Now, BeyondBurma tasty food can be found at a restaurant in downtown SF and catering to locations all over the Bay Area.
